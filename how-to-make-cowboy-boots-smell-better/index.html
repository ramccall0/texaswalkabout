How to make cowboy boots smell better? (Explained)

This is an easier fix than you might think. All you need to do is sprinkle baking soda all over the inside of both boots. Then allow it to sit for at least one night before wearing them. Another option is to use a commercial fabric softener sheet such as Dryel. Tuck one into the bottom of each boot. Let them sit overnight. Then wallah! You have freshened up boots!

Do leather cowboy boots really stink?

Sometimes yes. Brand, new leather can have an odor, although it should not be that unpleasant. Most of the time the stink is a result of the wearer's legs sweating inside the boots. This is especially true if he or she has been very active or if it is particularly hot outside.

Foot odor inside your cowboy boots can also come if you have been wearing the boots repeatedly without letting them air out properly. For the best defense against foot odor, allow your cowboy boots to be freshened up for a couple of days.

Are there any solutions to this?

Have you been wearing socks inside the boots? You really should be as it tends to negate any unpleasant odors from your feet. If you don't fancy full socks, you can purchase smaller foot pads that cover just the bottoms of your feet.

As far as socks themselves go, you can choose some that are padded. Most department stores carry them. Check with your local Walmart or Target. It is possible that stores where you buy the cowboy boots carry suitable socks or foot pads specifically made to be worn with the boots.

Cleaning your cowboy boots

It is important to clean the inside and outside of your boots after each use, particularly if it has been a long use. We gave you an idea of how to freshen up the insides with baking soda or fabric softener sheets. Did you know that if you never clean the outsides, odors can emanate from there too?

Mud and dirt can build up on the outer parts of the boots, not to mention if you have been cleaning horse or other animal poop while wearing them. Not only will this smell horrible, but it can ruin the boots. Do yourself a favor, clean your boots!

How to clean the outside of your cowboy boots

You can buy commercial leather cleaners specifically for cowboy boots. Here are some steps you can take:

  1. Moisten a rag, sponge, or soft brush with warm water
  2. Gently wipe down the entire boot
  3. While the rag and boots are still damp, apply the leather cleaner
  4. First apply the cleaner to the rag
  5. Then wipe down the boots using a minimal amount of the cleaner
  6. Rinse excess cleaner off the boots with tap water
  7. When finished, allow the boots to air dry naturally
  8. NEVER use a hair dryer as this will damage the boots
  9. If you are in a hurry to wear these boots, you should already own a special boot dryer for such occasions, use it to dry your boots

Can I prevent my boots from smelling?

It might not be possible to completely prevent this. So many factors are present when cowboy boots begin to smell. However, it should be able to be mitigated and eliminated quickly when they do smell. How? Keep reading!

By taking our advice for keeping your boots clean, you can lessen the smell that builds up after repeated use. If you have boots you use for dressy occasions, you ought to avoid using them to clean up a mess or when working. Use your alternative pairs of cowboy boots for those chores.

Getting rid of the smell of new cowboy boots

Sometimes bacteria or grime has accumulated all over the stitching associated with new, unworn boots. You can take a microfiber cloth or a soft-bristled brush to clean them.

Mix some water with white vinegar and clean the outside of your boots. Be sure to allow them to dry completely before wearing them. This will help you avoid the smell altogether.

Dealing with your smelly feet!

Why are we bringing this up? Because it could be a very real reason for the smell coming out of your cowboy boots. Whether you are a man or a woman, some people have incredibly stinky feet. You know who you are. If it is a chronic thing, you really should see a foot specialist.

If this happens to you after a long day of wearing your boots and is not a medical problem, there are things you can do. There are products available in stores and online that will help reduce the smell. Dr. Scholl's is widely-known for having such products. Other manufacturers probably exist as well. You can always ask a professional about what commercial remedy to buy.

Final thoughts

The problem of smelly cowboy boots is not an insurmountable one. There are many solutions. This means you can own a plethora of pairs of them and live happily ever after. This is great news for people who adore wearing cowboy boots. Enjoy them!
