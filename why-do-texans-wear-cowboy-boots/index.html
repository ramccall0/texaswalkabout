Why do texans wear cowboy boots? (Explained)

Cowboy Boots Texas Walkabout 03.23.2022
(Updated on 03.23.2022 )

Cowboy boots are a staple of the Texas aesthetic of deserts and cattle ranches. Cowboy boots date back to the 16th century when they functioned for riding horses and farming needs. Texans wore cowboy boots for riding horses, cattle drives, and other ranch work.

The cowboy boot has a heel that keeps the shoe in a stirrup for stability. The end of the boot is round at the toe for comfort. Their sturdy build and heels make them a perfect match for all cowboy needs.

Cowboy boots' functionality and purpose can make them quite dirty. People started owning two different types of boots. Texans owned a pair for their work and ranch needs and a flashy pair to pair with their street style. Fashionable cowboy boots led to different styles and colors of boots to go with any Texan aesthetic.

Are Cowboy Boots Popular in Texas?

Even though life in Texas is no longer an exact Western movie, cowboy boots are still popular. Texas has changed to adapt to modern life in the U.S., and some cities like Dallas and Austin have become artistic hubs. Even in modern-day Texas, many of the people in the state still wear cowboy boots for fashion and usefulness.

The cowboy boot has been passed down from generation to generation in Texas. Its history is in people's choice of shoes. You can see people wearing all types of cowboy boots in Texas, from different pointed toecaps, colors, to leather.

People of any gender wear cowboy boots. Even though the name is masculine, women and men alike wear cowboy boots. They are versatile and everlasting through the ages and changes of Texas.

What Was The Purpose of Cowboy Boots?

Cowboy boots are multifunctional for riding horses, herding cattle, and ranch work. They have been around for centuries, brought to Texas from Spain.

The original cowboy boot can be dated back to the Spanish Vaqueros. They were cowboys from Spain in the 1500s, the 16th century. When the Spanish came to Mexico and Texas, they herded cattle and rode horses. They left much of their traditions in Texas and Mexico and even spread to other parts of America. Mexican cowboy boots offered different leather skin and colors we see today.

In the 1860s, cowboy boots grew in popularity due to the industrial age. Texans began to wear them for their functionality and fashion.

Did Cowboys Really Wear Cowboy Boots?

Cowboys did wear cowboy boots and needed them for their abilities. From the 1860s to the 1890s, cowboys needed boots for cattle drives and riding horses. The original cowboy boot was not the best for cattle and ranch needs.

A bootmaker in Kansas began designing and building boots that the Texans were requesting. Slimmer, a higher heel, and a rounded toe. By the 1870s, Texans received new and improved cowboy boots just like they had asked for.

Do All Texans Wear Cowboy Boots?

In the present day and age, not every Texan wears cowboy boots. As fashion has changed and urban life has found itself in Texas, not all Texans wear cowboy fashion. Many Texans, however, still do.

Cowboy boots have been brought down from generation to generation in Texas. Many Texans wear them to represent Texas and their home. Other Texans wear them for their use in cattle farming and horse riding or racing.

What Do Texans Wear?

Texans wear modern, urban, and western fashion. Since the state is a mix of cultures of different people from all over the country and world, Texas has become a state with multiple styles mixing in one area.

When it comes to western fashion, Texans usually pair cowboy boots with jeans, a western button-down shirt, and a cowboy hat.

Final Thoughts

Like in the western days, cowboy boots are still just as popular in Texas. Even though Texas is more contemporary today, Texans still show their western history through their cowboy aesthetic.

Cowboy boots are versatile in their function. They provide comfort and sturdiness for cowboys on a cattle ranch or horse riding. They are also fashionable and often worn by people who do not involve themselves in western life. Cowboy boots will always have an everlasting impression on Texas fashion.
