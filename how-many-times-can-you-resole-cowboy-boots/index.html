How many times can you resole cowboy boots? (Explained) 

Cowboy Boots Texas Walkabout 03.23.2022
(Updated on 03.23.2022 )

It's normal for cowboy boots to become worn as time goes on. However, there's a good chance that the soles of your boots will wear out before the rest of the shoe does. When this happens, you may want to replace your old sole via a process called resoling.

If you resole your boots when they start to wear old, you can put off replacing those boots for a while. Resoling is a cost-effective way to protect your boots and keep them looking and feeling great.

How Many Times Can You Resole Cowboy Boots?

The number of times that a pair of cowboy boots can be resoled will vary based on the overall quality of the boots. Typically, boots can be resoled around two or three times. However, if your boots are still in good shape after the third resole, you may be able to resole them a fourth time.

Some manufacturers have limits on the amount of time they'll resole a pair of boots. Thankfully, even if you brush up against these kinds of limits, you'll still have options. Even if you can't have your boots resoled by the manufacturer, you could take them to a cobbler.

What Does It Mean to Resole a Pair of Cowboy Boots?

The outer sole of your boots may wear down more quickly than the rest of your boots. Resoling is a process that addresses that issue. When cowboy boots are resoled, the old, worn out sole of a boot will be detached from the shoe, and a new sole will be attached in its place.

Resoling is a way to extend the lifespan of a pair of boots that are mostly in good condition. Instead of paying for a brand-new pair of boots when the sole starts to wear down, you can just replace that old sole with a new one. Resoling can save you money, and it can also give you a way to keep wearing your favorite boots.

Should You Resole a Pair of Cowboy Boots?

Resoling is an excellent option for high-quality boots that are mostly in good condition. If you've invested in pricey cowboy boots, you should have your boots resoled as needed. For cheaper, low-quality boots, you may be better off replacing those old boots with something new.

Ultimately, it's up to you to decide if your boots should be resoled. If you're not happy with a pair of cowboy boots, and those boots have started to look worn, this could be an opportunity for you to upgrade to something better. If there are issues with the soles on your favorite cowboy boots, don't hesitate to have those boots resoled!

Why Would You Have to Resole a Pair of Cowboy Boots?

Even if you take excellent care of your boots, the soles will eventually wear down. This can cause all kinds of issues. For example, your boots might have uneven soles, or there might be a hole in the sole of one of your boots. If your boots are very worn, the sole might even start to split from the boot!

Cowboy boots are specifically designed to be durable. Most high-quality boots on the market today will have leather soles, which makes them more resistant to the elements. Boots are often made from high-quality leather and are constructed to provide feet with protection and support. However, even the best boots will have worn soles eventually. Resoling is a way to maintain boots so that they can be worn for longer.

Resoling isn't just about appearances. Boots with holes or faulty soles can be a safety hazard. Cowboy boots can be a way to reflect your personal style, but they're also a functional shoe. When you resole your boots, you're ensuring that they're providing your feet with the protection that they need.

Final Thoughts

When you wear cowboy boots regularly, the soles of the boots will start to wear down. Not only does this make boots less comfortable, but it can cause serious issues that make the boots unwearable, like holes. Thankfully, the boots can be resoled at least a few times, allowing you to wear them for longer.

Resoling isn't always your best option. When it comes to cheap cowboy boots, you might be better off investing in new boots that are more durable. With that said, resoling can be a fantastic way to save money, prevent further damage, and keep your favorite boots in good condition.
